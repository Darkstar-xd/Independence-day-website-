Our Journey to Freedom

1857 - First War of Independence

The Indian Rebellion of 1857 marked the beginning of organized resistance against British colonial rule, laying the foundation for future independence movements.

1885 - Formation of Indian National Congress

The Indian National Congress was established, providing a platform for political dialogue and the eventual struggle for independence.

1920 - Non-Cooperation Movement

Mahatma Gandhi launched the Non-Cooperation Movement, promoting non-violent resistance and civil disobedience against British rule.

1930 - Salt March

The historic Salt March led by Gandhi became a symbol of peaceful protest and civil disobedience, inspiring millions across the nation.

1942 - Quit India Movement

The Quit India Movement demanded an end to British rule, marking the final phase of the independence struggle.

August 15, 1947 - Independence Day

India finally achieved independence from British colonial rule, with Jawaharlal Nehru becoming the first Prime Minister of free India.
